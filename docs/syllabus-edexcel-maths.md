## Edexcel GCSE (1MA1) — Full Topic List by Grade Band

### Grade 1–2 (Foundation)
- Addition and Subtraction
- Angles (types, measuring, drawing, estimation)
- Area (rectangles, triangles, basic shapes)
- Averages (mean, mode, median, range — from a list)
- BIDMAS / Order of Operations
- Factors and Multiples
- Fractions of an Amount
- Fractions, Decimals and Percentages (FDP conversions)
- Frequency Polygons
- Multiplication and Division
- Negative Numbers
- Pie Charts
- Powers and Roots
- Simplifying Algebra (collecting like terms, basic notation)
- Stem and Leaf Diagrams

### Grade 3 (Foundation)
- Area and Circumference of Circles
- Area of Compound Shapes
- Best Buy / Value for Money
- Drawing Graphs (linear)
- Exchange Rates
- Fractions (operations: add, subtract, multiply, divide)
- Percentages (of an amount, increase/decrease)
- Probability (basic: single event)
- Proportion (direct)
- Ratio (simplifying, sharing)
- Solving Equations (unknown on both sides)
- Substitution into formulae
- Transformations: Reflections, Rotations, Translations, Enlargements

### Grade 4 (Foundation/Higher boundary)
- Angle Problems (multi-step)
- Angles in Parallel Lines (alternate, corresponding, co-interior)
- Angles in Polygons (interior/exterior)
- Averages from Frequency Tables
- Bearings
- Compound Interest and Depreciation
- Cylinders (volume, surface area)
- Expanding and Factorising (single brackets)
- Forming and Solving Equations
- HCF and LCM
- Indices (positive integer powers)
- Inequalities (solve and represent on number line)
- Inequalities on Graphs
- Loci and Constructions
- Probability (relative frequency, expected outcomes)
- Pythagoras' Theorem
- Scatter Graphs (correlation, line of best fit)
- Sequences (nth term of arithmetic sequence)
- Surface Area (prisms)
- Volume of Prisms

### Grade 5 (Higher)
- Changing the Subject of a Formula
- Congruent Triangles
- Drawing Quadratic Graphs
- Drawing Other Graphs (cubic, reciprocal)
- Equation of a Line (y = mx + c)
- Expanding and Factorising Quadratics (a=1)
- Probability Trees
- Reverse Percentages
- SOHCAHTOA (right-angled trigonometry)
- Sector Areas and Arc Lengths
- Similar Shapes (lengths only)
- Simultaneous Equations (linear, elimination/substitution)
- Solving Quadratics (by factorising)
- Speed, Distance, Time / Density, Mass, Volume
- Spheres and Cones (volume, surface area)
- Standard Form
- Venn Diagrams

### Grade 6 (Higher)
- Circle Theorems (6 main theorems)
- Cumulative Frequency and Box Plots
- Enlargements with Negative Scale Factor
- Fractional and Negative Indices
- Recurring Decimals to Fractions
- Similar Shapes (area and volume ratios)

### Grade 7 (Higher)
- 3D Pythagoras
- Algebraic Fractions (simplify, add, subtract, multiply)
- Bounds (upper/lower, error intervals)
- Direct and Inverse Proportion (y ∝ xⁿ)
- Harder Graphs (trigonometric, exponential)
- Histograms (unequal class widths, frequency density)
- Inverse and Composite Functions
- Quadratic Formula
- Rearranging Harder Formulae (subject appears twice)
- Sine Rule, Cosine Rule, Area of Any Triangle
- Solving Equations using Iteration
- Surds (simplify, rationalise denominator)
- Vectors
- Venn Diagrams (conditional probability / "given that")

### Grade 8–9 (Higher)
- Completing the Square
- Perpendicular Lines and Equation of Tangent
- Proof (algebraic)
- Proof of Circle Theorems
- Quadratic Inequalities
- Simultaneous Equations (one linear, one quadratic)
- nth Term of a Quadratic Sequence
- Transforming Graphs y = f(x) (translations, reflections, stretches)
- Velocity-Time Graphs (area under curve = distance)

## Edexcel IGCSE (4MA1) — Topic List

The IGCSE broadly covers the same ground as GCSE Higher but with some differences:

### Number
- Integers, decimals, fractions, percentages
- Sets (notation, union, intersection, Venn diagrams) — *formally tested, unlike GCSE*
- Standard form
- Estimation and rounding
- Limits of accuracy (bounds)
- Ratio and proportion
- Financial mathematics (simple/compound interest, profit/loss, VAT)
- Calculator skills

### Algebra
- Graphs in practical situations (distance-time, speed-time, conversion)
- Graphs of functions (linear, quadratic, cubic, reciprocal, exponential)
- Algebraic representation and formulae (substitution, rearranging)
- Algebraic manipulation (expand, factorise, simplify)
- Functions (notation, inverse, composite)
- Indices (laws, fractional, negative)
- Solutions of equations and inequalities (linear, quadratic, simultaneous)
- Linear programming (Higher) — *not in GCSE*
- Sequences (arithmetic, geometric, nth term)

### Geometry and Measures
- Angles (parallel lines, polygons, bearings)
- Mensuration (perimeter, area, volume — all standard shapes)
- Trigonometry (SOHCAHTOA, sine/cosine rule, exact values)
- Pythagoras (2D and 3D)
- Circle theorems
- Similarity and congruence
- Transformations (reflection, rotation, translation, enlargement)
- Vectors in two dimensions
- Loci and constructions

### Statistics and Probability
- Data collection, sampling
- Averages and spread (mean, median, mode, range, IQR)
- Statistical diagrams (bar, pie, histogram, cumulative frequency, box plot, scatter)
- Correlation and lines of best fit
- Probability (single, combined, tree diagrams, conditional)

### Higher only (IGCSE, not in GCSE)
- Differential calculus (differentiation, gradient of curve, stationary points) — *unique to IGCSE Higher*
- Linear programming

## Key Differences: GCSE vs IGCSE

| Feature | GCSE (1MA1) | IGCSE (4MA1) |
|---|---|---|
| Exam papers | 3 × 1h30m (1 non-calc + 2 calc) | 2 × 2h (both calculator) |
| Grading | 1–9 | 1–9 (same scale) |
| Sets | Informal only | Formally tested |
| Calculus | Not included | Higher tier only |
| Linear programming | Not included | Higher tier |
| UK curriculum alignment | England national curriculum | International |
| Assessment weighting | ~22% Number, ~30% Algebra (Higher) | More evenly spread |
